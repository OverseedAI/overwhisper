Workbench

This is the unglamorous stuff that makes dictation usable.

A dictation app lives or dies on the boring edges: hotkeys, microphones, silence, retries, cleanup, and getting out of your way.

Hotkeys

Toggle or push-to-talk

Tap Option+Space to toggle, or hold Option+Shift+Space to record only while pressed. Both are configurable.

Microphones

Choose the right input

Use the system default microphone or pick a specific device when your desk setup gets complicated.

Cleanup

Vocabulary and replacements

Bias names and acronyms, then fix predictable mistakes like Cloud Code to Claude Code automatically.

Recovery

History, playback, retry

Copy recent transcripts, inspect local debug sessions, play back captured audio, and retry failures.

Guardrails

Skip silence and limit runs

Ignore accidental empty recordings and set a duration limit for the times you forget recording is on.

Feedback

Overlay without theater

A compact waveform shows that recording is live, then gets out of the way when transcription is done.

Flow

Four moves. No transcript janitor work.

1

Focus any field

Messages, Notes, your browser, an IDE, a ticket, a doc, wherever text goes.

2

Press the hotkey

Toggle recording or use push-to-talk. The menu bar app stays quiet until needed.

3

Say the thing

Local models transcribe on your Mac, with optional language hints and translation.

4

Keep moving

The text is pasted at your cursor. If Accessibility is missing, it is copied to clipboard.

Your voice should not need a server trip.

With WhisperKit or Parakeet, audio stays on your Mac. OpenAI is used only when selected, or when you enable fallback and local transcription fails.

Recent debug sessions are stored locally so retry and troubleshooting work. They are capped and clearable from Settings. API keys live in Keychain.

Use your Mac like it has a microphone and a Neural Engine.

Overwhisper is free, open source, and built for people who want real dictation value without another subscription meter running in the background.
